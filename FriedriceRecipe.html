FRIED RICE RECIPE

Fried rice is a popular Asian dish made by stir-frying cooked rice with vegetables, protein (like eggs, chicken, or shrimp), and seasonings. It originated in China but has many regional variations across Asia (e.g., Thai, Indonesian, Japanese versions).

Common Ingredients

Here’s a typical ingredient list for basic fried rice (serves 2–3 people):

  • 2 cups cooked rice(preferably a day old)
  • 2 tablespoons oil (vegetable, sesame, or peanut oil)
  • 2 eggs (beaten)
  • ½ cup diced onion
  • 1 clove garlic (minced)
  • ½ cup peas and carrots (frozen mix)
  • 2–3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Optional protein: diced cooked chicken, shrimp, tofu, or pork
  • Optional garnish: chopped green onions or sesame seeds

Steps to Make Fried Rice

  • Prepare ingredients: Dice vegetables and protein, beat the eggs, and ensure rice is chilled and separated.
  • Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  • Scramble eggs: Push to the side or remove when cooked.
  • Sauté onions and garlic until fragrant.
  • Add vegetables and stir-fry until tender.
  • Add rice and stir-fry, breaking up clumps.
  • Return eggs and protein to the pan.
  • Add sauces (soy sauce, oyster sauce) and mix well.
  • Taste and season with salt and pepper.
  • Serve hot, garnished with green onions or sesame seeds.
